Goth Fashion

Gothic

The term goth, short for gothic, was used at the beginning of the 1980s to describe specific rebellious youths who had an alternative view of the world and a distinctive way of dressing. The term gothic has been previously used to describe the macabre and shadowy atmospheres of medieval Northern European architecture. The dark aesthetic attracted many young people to express themselves in ways relating to that. Some famous goths include Siouxsie Sioux, The Addams Family, Robert Smith, and Alice Cooper.





Goth, being a subculture, takes a lot of inspiration from gothic rock and offshoots from the punk movement of the 1970s. There are many sub-genres under the gothic fashion such as deathrock, cybergoth, traditional goth, and victorian goth. In preference to darkness, goth attire has so many different looks that can be attributed to the style. Typically seen in regards to dressing are dark clothing, black velvet, lace, fishnets, leather, and corsets. Accessories can be spiked collars or heavy ankh pendants, jewelled chokers, silk cravats, or silver jewelry. Goth individuals may also have piercings.

Fashion is not just clothing which is very apparent in gothic fashion. Following the theme of darkness, traditional goth individuals wore dark heavy eyeliner, black lipstick, and pale bases often through the use of lighter foundation. Makeup generally expressed the choice to create something new, showing mystique and allure. Gothic makeup often expresses emotions like sadness, desperation or loneliness. In this makeup style, there are no standard colours or shapes to put on your face. Hairstyles are typically dark dyed, heavily layered hairstyles with bangs of all kinds.





Hair is typically dark dyed, heavily layered hairstyles with bangs of all kinds bangs. Many sport mohawks/fauxhawks, fringes, braids, twists, and messy teased hair.
