1920's Fashion

The Roaring 20's

At the dawn of the decade, the world was still shaken from the First World War. The recently concluded conflict had a significant and irreversible effect on society, culture, and fashion. Fundamental to these new styles was a simplicity that had yet to be seen in women’s fashion. Fashion in this era is often typified by flappers, jazz, "boyish" silhouettes, and glamour.


“The development of a more convenient, modern female wardrobe was a major trend of the 1920's and was achieved through the progressive simplification of dress as the decade advanced – a rejection of formality and multiple layers, in favour of comfort and a lighter, more natural effect.” (13-14, Fashion in the 1920s) - Jayne Shrimpton


A major focus was how the body itself was fashioned, rejecting the uncomfortability of figure shapers in prior years. Simple lines and androgynous shapes looked best. This created the popular tubular “la garçonne” look that dominated most of the ‘20s. The flapper-a popular look that often characterized 1920s dressing- was a dropped waist and creeping hemlines that could be produced in economical fabrics. Coco Chanel was significantly important in the popularization of this style, becoming a well-known and successful designer. Hemlines and Waistlines would continue to shorten and lengthen throughout the decade. Women had bobbed hair and accessorized with cloche hats, long necklaces, and short heels.
