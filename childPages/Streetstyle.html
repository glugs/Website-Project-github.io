Streetwear

Hence the name, streetwear is a style of casual "street" clothing. It has always existed, but the modern form of fashion originated in the mid-1950s. Streetstyle has been an important factor in many social movements. Streetstyle is not definite as the whole point is to intersect, and diverge from mainstream fashion thinking. Usually found in urban city centres, Streetwear looks different globally. Streetstyle has a lot of varieties such as athleisure, skatewear, teach, wear, and luxury. Luxury is rather new as designers such as Virgil Abloh.



The more common style of Streetwear we know today originated in the '90s hip-hop scenes of New York, the surf-skate and graffiti culture of Los Angeles, and within the nightlife of Japan. Brands like BAPE, Supreme, Stussy, Kith are some of the "originals". They utilize their popularity to keep demand high by dropping exclusive items in scarce quantities. Being that the style is a reflection of pop culture, celebrity influence plays a big role in streetwear with artists like Kayne West, A$ap Rocky, Bella Hadid, and Tyler The Creator.

Streetstyle has a lot of varieties such as athleisure, adopted, skatewear, techwear, and luxury. Luxury is rather new, as designers such as Virgil Abloh-the creator of Of-White-, Rick Owens, and Raf Simmons, have merged streetwear with the realm of high fashion. Other "original" brands have also done high fashion collaborations


Streetwear can basically be anything you want it to be, mixing practicality with self-expression. The typical pieces you'll find under this style are t-shirts, hoodies, sweatpants, and sneakers. Sneaker culture is a particularly large aspect of streetwear.
