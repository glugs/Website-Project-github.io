Fast Fashion

Fast fashion is the design, manufaturing, and marketing method that allows for rapid production and high volumes of clothing. The latest catwalk trends are able to hit stores in a matter of days.It utilizes trend replication and low-quality materials like synthetic fabrics, to bring inexpensive clothing to the public. Unfortunatley the low cost of clothing does not tranlate in other ways.


Fast fashion can be dated back to the British Industrial revolution where high demand required cheap and high supply. Workers as young as 4 years old worked abusivley long hours for bare pay. Something as exploitative as this would never happen in modern society, right? Wrong. Fast fashion has only encourged this to continue. In countries like Bangladesh, China, and India, labour laws are next to nothing. This allows companies to go in obtain cheap and labour.

With trends nowadays coming and going with such speed, companies need to keep up if they want to survive. Production in countries like Canada and the U.S. would result in in low profit margins which is why they seek places like Bangladesh and China. Working conditions are inhumane, some people work for as little as 3 US cents per hour, often more than 100 hours per week in conditions of poor air quaility and extreme heat. Some of the most unethical companies include: Nike, Shien, Boohoo, Fashion Nova, Adidas, H&M, The Walt Disney Company, Zara Forever 21, and the goes on and on.


Not only are people being exploited, but the enviroment. The enviromental impact of fast fashion is incredibly detrimental. To put this into perspective, it takes 10,000 liters of water to produce one kilogram of cotton or approximately 3,000 liters of water for one cotton shirt.and is responsible for around 20% of industrial water pollution. Becuase of the rapid consumation of buyers these garments are often thrown out after a mere 14 wears(on average). The synthetic materials are ulimately unable to decompose, while simultaneously releasing microfibers and toxins.

What can we do?

With all the devastating consequences things don't look so good. Fortunately, there are a few things you can do to help. First refrain from buy unethically made products. This will encourge companies be more sustaiable and ethical. When purchasing, choose ethical and sustainable brands like: Patagonia, Levi's, Artizia, Uniqlo, and Zara-Yup they've changed-. Thrifting is also another method as clothes are being recycled rather than thrown out. Be concious about your shopping habits, so everyone can have fun with fashion.
