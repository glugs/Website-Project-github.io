2000s

Y2k

Aside from the threat of impending digital death, the year 2000 was a funky year in pop culture and fashion. Many fashion trends in the 2000s were a result of globalization, the rise of fast fashion-affordable clothes based off runway designs usually found in department stores like Dillard's, J.C. Penney, and Macy’s-, and celebrities’ growing influence as style icons(Paris Hilton, Amanda Bynes, The Olsen Twins, Rhianna, etc.). As with all decades, looks and trends varied. Styles generally hit big and burnt out fast.



Movies and T.V. shows of the decade heavily influenced fashion. Iconic characters like Cher and Dionne, Elle Woods, Raven Baxter, Lizzy Mcguire, and Blair Waldorf were fashion icons. Popular to take inspiration from was: The O.C., 90210, Disney Chanel originals, as well as "chick flicks" like Legally Blonde, 13 Going on 30, She's The Man, Clueless, and John Tucker must die.


Early 2000s fashion was significantly influenced by Technology and Y2K. Colour palettes were filled with shiny black tones and reflective metallics. Popular outfits included mesh or handkerchief tops, box-pleated or leather skirts, shiny pants, and sparkly shoes. Britney Spears is a definite trendsetter for this style Casual clothing and leisurewear were the other big trends of the early 2000s. Denim became a staple, think crop tops, hoodies, low-rise flared jeans, cargo pants, jean/mini skirts, ribbed sweaters, with platform sandals, Ugg boots, or sneakers.


The middle of the decade was also defined by the latest "it" items, like Von Dutch trucker hats, Juicy Couture tracksuits, and Louis Vuitton bags. Tracksuits were often brightly coloured and emblazoned with rhinestone logos and phrases. It was also popular to mix track pants with dress clothing and designer shoes to elevate the look. Hip Hop, Emo, streetwear, scene styles were also prevalent.
